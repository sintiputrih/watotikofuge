Did Ray Liotta Have Parkinsons? Cause of Death Not Clear, Goodfellas Icon Dies In Dominican Repub

Did Ray Liotta Have Parkinson’s? Raymond Allen Liotta was an actor and producer from the United States. He is most known for his roles in the films Field of Dreams (1989), Goodfellas (1990), and Grand Theft Auto: Vice City (video game) (2002).

Liotta was born in Newark, New Jersey, and was adopted at the age of six months by Mary, the township clerk, and Alfred Liotta, the proprietor of an auto parts company.

Liotta graduated from Union High School in 1973 and went on to study acting at the University of Miami, earning a BFA in 1978.

He moved to New York City after graduating from college and found a job as a bartender at the Shubert Organization, as well as an agency, within six months.

One of Liotta’s earliest roles was as Joey Perrini on the soap opera, “Another World”.

He however quit the show so he could try his luck in the film industry and went to Los Angeles.

Liotta made his film debut in the 1983 film, “The Lonely Lady”. He got his first major acting role in, “Something Wild” (1986), which earned him his first Golden Globe nomination, the nomination was for Best Supporting Actor – Motion Picture.

Did Ray Liotta Have Parkinson’s?

In his lifetime, the actor did not reveal much about his health. It is therefore unknown whether he had Parkinson’s disease or not. Liotta passed away in his sleep on, Tuesday, May 26, 2022, while in the Dominican Republic filming “Dangerous Waters”.

Ray Liotta’s Cause of Death

Ray Liotta, who was best remembered for his role as Henry Hill in the American classic Goodfellas, died at the age of 67.

According to Deadline, Liotta went unexpectedly in his sleep in the Dominican Republic while filming his upcoming picture Dangerous Waters. There are no other facts concerning Liotta’s death, including the cause of death, available at this time.

In 2021, Liotta acted in two films: No Sudden Move and The Many Saints of Newark, proving that he was still as busy as ever. He’s also working on two projects that will be released later this year: Elizabeth Banks’ Cocaine Bear and an untitled feature directed by Charlie Day of It’s Always Sunny.

During this terrible time, our thoughts and prayers are with Mr. Liotta’s friends, family, and loved ones.

Why is Robert De Niro projected as the actor in the movie “Goodfellas” rather than Ray Liotta who seems to be the central character of the plot?

… it showcases Robert De Niro in the middle, with his name higher than Joe Pesci and Ray Liotta. This is a contract maneuver, which is very common. De Niro was obviously the bigger name. He was the top billed actor. It’s obvious that his contract for the film dictated that he get top billing. So in that context, you can see why he was billed as the lead.

Beyond that, I agree 100% as far as the fact that Ray Liotta IS the lead character in the film. And it’s a damn shame the Academy didn’t recognize him with an Oscar nomination. He wasn’t nominated for any notable awards at all. It shocks me. To me, it was perhaps one of the biggest Oscar snubs of all time.

As to why De Niro was nominated for a BAFTA as Lead Actor? The studio generally decides how they want to campaign their various actors for Awards season. If you read Variety, you’ll often see the For Your Consideration ads which basically announce how the studio would like voters to vote for each actor. They likely chose Best Actor for De Niro because of his top billing and perhaps because he is so connected as leads for Martin Scorsese films.

But I agree. Liotta was vastly underappreciated in the role. Without his performance, there is no film.
