Does Sheinelle Jones Have Siblings? Journalists Family Life Explored

NBC News correspondent and journalist Sheinelle Jones is the daughter of U.S. District Court Judge C. Darnell Jones II. Does she have siblings or is she the only child?

Sheinelle Jones is a successful and well-respected American journalist. A native of Philadelphia, she co-hosts the NBC’s show Today on weekdays.

Jones started her career with WICS in Springfield, Illinois. She then went on to work at KOKI-TV, WTXF-TV, the FOX O&O, and the FOX affiliate in Tulsa, Oklahoma.

She started working for NBC News as an anchor on Today’s show in 2014. She was a part of Weekday Today. After five years, she started co-hosting 3rd Hour Today.

The journalist was recently inducted into the Hall of Achievement at Northwestern University’s Medill School on Thursday (November 4), reported Hello!.

Jones also took to Instagram to share the news with her 351K followers. She posted an almost 4-minute video where she expressed her gratitude for the honor she received.

In the caption, she called it a ‘Thankful Thursday’ post.

Does Sheinelle Jones Have Siblings? Journalist’s Family Life Explored

No, Sheinelle Jones does not have any siblings. She is the only child of her parents.

The University of Illinois graduate is known as the daughter of C. Darnell Jones II, the senior United States District Judge, and Sheila Kinnard, a retired drama teacher.

Her father, C. Darnell was born in Claremore, Oklahoma. The 71-year-old received his Juris Doctor from the American University Washington College of Law in 1975.

He assumed the post of U.S District Court Judge on October 30, 2008, and achieved senior status in March 2021.
