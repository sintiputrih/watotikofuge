Tony Hale | Bio, Age, Net Worth, Relationship, Career

Tony Hale is an American actor, comedian, voiceover artist, and producer based in Los Angeles. He has been honored with two Primetime Emmy Awards.

His performance as Buster Bluth on the Fox comedy series Arrested Development is where he is most widely recognized. The actor also portrayed Gary Walsh on the HBO series Veep.

Alvin and the Chipmunks: The Road Chip, The Informant!, In My Sleep, and The Heat are among the feature films in which Tony has appeared.

Where was Tony Hale born? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

On September 30, 1970, Anthony Russell Hale was born in West Point, New York, as Tony Hale. Mike Hale and Rita (née Garnett) are his parents.

His father is a former military specialist in nuclear and atomic physics, and his mother is State Representative Kathy Ashe’s administrative assistant.

During his childhood in Tallahassee, Florida, he participated in several theatrical and musical productions at the Young Actors Theatre. The height of 6 feet 1 inch (185 centimeters) is his stature. In 2021, Tony Hale will turn fifty years old. He holds American citizenship and is of Caucasian ancestry.

Hale graduated from Leon High School with the completion of his academic career in 1988. He subsequently attended Samford University in Birmingham, Alabama, from which he graduated in 1992 with a degree in journalism.

Tony Hale graduated from the School of Communication and the Arts at Regent University in 1995. He subsequently moved to New York City to attend the Barrow Group for acting studies. He also participated in the William Esper Studio’s Professional Actor Training Program.

Vows were exchanged between Tony Hale and Martel Thompson on May 24, 2003. His wife is an Emmy-winning makeup artist.

How did Tony Hale start his Professional Career?

Hale has appeared in several feature films and television series, including Chuck, ER, Numbers, and The Informant!, In My Sleep, The Heat, and Alvin and the Chipmunks: The Road Chip.

Gary Walsh played his role from 2012 to 2019 on the HBO sitcom Veep. His work earned him two Primetime Emmy Awards in 2013 and 2015, each for Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Comedy Series.

Tony also supplied the voice of Forky in the animated comedy film Toy Story 4, which debuted at number one at the box office in 2019.
