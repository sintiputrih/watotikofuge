The capacity of a Glock pistol varies depending on the specific model and the calibre of ammunition it is designed to use. Glock offers a range of models in different calibres, and each model may have various magazine options that affect the capacity. However, some common examples include:

  • Glock 17: The Glock 17, chambered in 9mm, typically has a standard magazine capacity of 17 rounds.
  • Glock 19: The Glock 19, another popular 9mm model, usually has a standard magazine capacity of 15 rounds.
  • Glock 22: In .40 S&W caliber, the Glock 22 often comes with a standard magazine capacity of 15 rounds.
  • Glock 23: Similar to the Glock 22 but in a more compact size, the Glock 23 also typically has a standard magazine capacity of 13 rounds in .40 S&W.
  • Glock 26: The subcompact Glock 26, chambered in 9mm, usually has a standard magazine capacity of 10 rounds.
