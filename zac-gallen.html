Zac Gallen Bio, Age, Height, Girlfriend, Parents, Net Worth

Zac Gallen, in full, Zachary Peter Gallen, is an American personality and a professional baseballer. He plays as a pitcher for the Arizona Diamondbacks of Major League Baseball (MLB). He played college baseball for nearly three seasons for the North Carolina Tar Heels. Later, he was drafted by the St. Louis Cardinals in the 2016 MLB draft in the third round.

After playing for one season for the St. Louis Cardinals, he was traded to the Miami Marlins in 2017. During the 2019 season, he was transferred to the Diamondbacks. In 2023, he was named the starting pitcher for the All-Star Games of the National League.

Gallen was born on 3rd August 1995 in Somerdale, New Jersey, to his loving and caring parents, Jim and Stacy Gallen. When he was five years old, he declined the decision to play in the tee-ball team with his peers. Instead, he demanded to play Little League Baseball with the Somerdale team.

Eventually, he was taken by his father’s team to the third round of the Little League draft. It was decided that he would now play with children between seven and nine years old. After that, at 11, Gallen joined the Tri-state Arsenal Baseball Academy, where he started playing as a pitcher and second baseman.

Then, he joined Bishop Eustace Preparatory School in Pennsauken Township and the University of North Carolina. At the University of North Carolina, he played baseball continuously for three years. During his junior year 2016, he made a 4-3 [win-loss] record with a 2.79 ERA [Earned Run Average].

Career

Initially, Zac Gallen was drafted by the St. Louis Cardinals in the third round of the MLB Draft 2016. While being with the Cardinals, he was promoted to different teams. He made his professional debut with the Gulf Coast Cardinals, then started the 2017 season with the Palm Beach Cardinals and was later promoted to the Springfield Cardinals and Memphis Redbirds.

In December 2017, he was traded to the Miami Marlins as a non-roster invitee to 2018 spring training. For the first season, he spent his time and energy for New Orleans Baby Cakes, where he scored 8-9 with an ERA of 3.65 in 25 starts.

At the beginning of 2019, he returned to New Orleans, and then in June 2019, he was promoted to the major leagues by the Marlins. The same night, he had his debut against the Cardinals recorded six strikeouts, and gave up one earned run over five innings pitched.

On 31st July 2019, Zac Gallen was traded to the Arizona Diamondbacks. For the initial period, he had eight starts for Arizona and had a record of 2 – 3 with an ERA of 2.89 in 43+2⁄3 innings, striking out 53. Then, in 2020, he recorded 82 strikeouts in 72 innings and finished the season with an ERA of 2.75 and a record of 3 – 2.

In January 2023, Zac Gallen made a contract for one year with the Diamondbacks while getting agreed on the contractual amount of $5.6 million for one year without any salary arbitration. He was named NL Player of the Week from 10 to 16th April.
