Here's Why Fans Are Excited For George Clooney's 'Boys In The Boat'

Who doesn’t love an underdog story? Add a come from behind victory, and it’s pure satisfaction. That’s the premise of the upcoming film The Boys in the Boat, in the works since 2011 and is directed by award-winning actor George Clooney.Not much has been said about the historical drama except for some revelation about casting. Due to limited information, fans are on their toes on what to expect from this exciting movie. The film is based on the nonfiction book of Daniel James Brown titled The Boys in the Boat: Nine Americans and Their Epic Quest for Gold at the 1936 Berlin Olympics.The novel, published by Penguin Books in 2013, is about the victory of the University of Washington rowing team who represented the United States in the 1936 Olympic Games in Berlin, Germany – a golden feat for the country at a time when many are struggling because of the Great Depression.The inspiring story is fit for Hollywood and fans are excited about this period drama.

What Is 'Boys In The Boat' About?

The book’s main character is Joseph ‘Joe’ Rantz. He was interviewed by the author, Brown, upon learning that he was the father of his neighbor. That neighbor approached Brown to tell him that his dad is a fan of his works and wanted to meet him. Rantz shared to the writer his experiences during the economic depression and his stint as a UW rower.

For the book, Brown focused on the preparation of the team before the big game. Alongside Rantz were rowers Herbert Morris, Charles Day, Gordon Adam, John White, James ‘Stub’ McMillin, George ‘Shorty’ Hunt, Donald Hume, and coxswain Robert Moch. They were coached by Al Ulbrickson.

It’s expected that much of the movie will focus on the build-up to the race, just like the Brown narration. The film might also employ the two backstories of the novel: the first one being the struggles of the nine students during the economic crisis, which threatened their education.

The second is about Nazi Germany's construction of the Olympic venues while they allegedly covered up their abuses against the Jews to curry favor from the international community as they organize the sporting event.

Production And Release Date

The Weinstein Company acquired the film rights for the book as early as 2011, even before its publication two years later. It was originally laid on the table of award-winning director Kenneth Branagh, who is directing a sci-fi movie, and producer Donna Gigliotti.

It remained in limbo until 2018 when Lantern Entertainment (successor of The Weinstein Company) partnered with Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer for the movie's distribution. Two more years without an update, it was announced that Clooney will take over as director. 2021 saw Clooney busy on the director’s chair of The Tender Bar, so The Boys in the Boat remained in the backseat.

However, that same year saw more names popping up as part of the movie: Clooney’s co-director is his Smokehouse Pictures partner Grant Heslov, Mark L. Smith will work on the screenplay, and Chris Weitz revisited a previous draft. SpyGlass is the executive producer. Moreover, British actor Callum Turner was announced as the first star to be cast.

Since the movie is still deep in development, there's no mention of a release date, but it is expected to be in two years, maybe 2023. This, while Clooney is still busy with the romantic comedy film Ticket to Paradise, which is set to be released in late 2022. And nobody knows if the production team and cast are already complete.

Who Will Be In 'Boys In The Boat'?

It is not yet known if Callum Turner will be playing the main character. The BAFTA-nominated actor had his breakout role in the thriller Green Room. According to Turner's IMDB profile, he has appeared in numerous films like Assassin's Creed, Victor Frankenstein, and Fantastic Beasts: The Crimes of Grindelwald, among others.

February 2022 ushered in new updates for the film. Joining Turner are Hadley Robinson, Joel Edgerton, Wil Coban, Jack Mulhern, Bruce Herbelin-Earle, Sam Strike, Tom Varey, Thomas Elms, and Luke Slattery.

Robinson is known for her roles in the blockbuster hit Little Women, television show Utopia, and Moxie. Meanwhile, Edgerton, who is the veteran among the cast, starred in a couple of Star Wars films, The Great Gatsby, King Arthur (which actually lost Warner Bros money), and Zero Dark Thirty. The Australian was also the director of the award-winning The Gift.

Coban was recently part of Zack Snyder’s Justice League while Mulhern starred in the HBO series Mare of Eastown. Another young actor in the mix is model Herbelin-Earle, known for his stint in Netflix drama Free Rein. English actor Strike, on the other hand, is best known for his role in the BBC series EastEnders.

Ridley Road's Varey, Elms of Timeless, and Slattery from Steven Spielberg’s The Post are all part of the crew. March 2022 revealed two more celebrities joining the team: Courtney Henggeler and James Wolk.

Henggeler was part of the TV series The Big Bang Theory, Jane the Virgin, and Cobra Kai, plus movies Friends with Benefits and Nobody's Fool. Wolk, however, is known for his roles in The Crazy Ones, Mad Men, Zoo, and Watchmen. The team is getting packed, so expect more revelations from the production.

A documentary was already made in 2016 about the rowing team, giving the public not just a snapshot of their feat but taking viewers to the challenging and hostile situation the boys endured just so they can bring that gold home. The movie will help relive that moment of American pride, proving once more that victory is achieved through a determined spirit.
