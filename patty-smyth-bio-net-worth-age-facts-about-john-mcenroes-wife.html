Patty Smyth’s biography

The Grammy nominee was born Patricia Smythin New York City, USA on June 26, 1957 to her parents George and Betty Smyth. She grew up in Gerritsen Beach, Brooklyn, along with her older sister, Elizabeth. Regarding her academic performance, Patty Smyth has not released any information about it as it is still not known which high school she attended.

At a young age, the Oscar nominee decided that she would be a singer. At the age of 15, Smyth began performing Cat Stevens’ songs in Folk City, New York, playing musical sets at the Catch A Rising Star comedy club. She then formed her first band called Patty and the Planets. After answering the call to become the lead singer of Zack Smith’s rock band Scandal in 1981, Patty Smyth hasn’t looked back since as she took her music career to greater heights.

The following year, the band released their first album entitled “EP” and one of their songs “Goodbye To You” which peaked at #5 on the Billboard Album Rock chart. The success of their first album led to the release of their second album entitled “Warrior” on August 21, 1984. The album peaked at No. 17 on the US Billboard Hot 200 albums chart, while its title track “The Warrior” reached No. 1 on the US Billboard Album Rock Tracks chart and Canadian RPM Top Singles. It also ranked seventh on the US Billboard Hot 100 chart and won a BMI Airplay Award.

After deciding to break up the band in Berlin in 1985, Patty Smyth started her solo career and released her first album “Never Enough” in 1987. The album was a success as it took fourth place on US Mainstream Rock Tracks. As a result of the success of their earlier venture, Smyth returned on August 18, 1992 with their second official music album, ‘Patty Smyth’. It’s a single entitled ‘Sometimes Love Just Isn’t Enough’ earned her a Grammy nomination. Her song “Look What Love Has Done,” which was the theme song to the 1994 film, Junior , also earned her Grammy and Oscar nominations.

The defunct rock band Scandal reunited in 2004 and released the single “Hard For You To Love Me” on January 17, 2009 in Ridgefield, Connecticut.
