Former NFL Player Sergio Brown is Missing &amp; His Mother Found Dead

The disturbing disappearance of former NFL defensive back Sergio Brown has taken a tragic turn with the death of his mother, deepening the mystery surrounding the former pro athlete’s unknown whereabouts.

Sergio Brown, 35, was a standout high school football player at Proviso East in Maywood, Illinois prior to playing college football at Notre Dame from 2005-2009. He went on to play professionally with the New England Patriots, Indianapolis Colts, Jacksonville Jaguars and Buffalo Bills from 2010-2016.

Brown had been a beloved member of the Maywood community, known as a hard-working and charismatic young man dedicated to improving his hometown. Which makes his sudden disappearance all the more troubling for loved ones and authorities.

Brown was last seen on September 15 in Maywood, with family members reporting him missing shortly thereafter. Tragically, on the same day Brown vanished, the body of his mother Myrtle Brown was discovered in Addison Creek near her Maywood home. The cause of her death remains under investigation.

The uncanny timing has compounded the mystery of Brown’s disappearance, leaving friends and family reeling. Searches of Addison Creek and surrounding areas by law enforcement and volunteers have so far yielded no clues to Brown’s whereabouts.

Loved ones describe Brown and his mother Myrtle as having an extremely close bond. Myrtle was a beloved figure in Maywood, admired for her vibrant spirit and dedication to community service. That both mother and son have disappeared and died under such puzzling circumstances has left residents shaken and authorities probing every angle.

At this stage, investigators have not ruled out any possibilities in the dual tragedies, urging anyone with information to come forward. They have been combing through surveillance footage and tracing Brown’s digital footprint for leads.

Brown’s former NFL teams have also expressed shock and concern over his disappearance, hoping for his safe return. As word has spread nationally, NFL players who knew Brown have taken to social media asking fans for potential information.
