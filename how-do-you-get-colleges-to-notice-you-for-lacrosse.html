How do you get colleges to notice you for lacrosse?

Lacrosse is an exciting and competitive sport that requires dedication, hard work, and skill. If you aspire to play lacrosse at the college level, you need to take proactive steps to get noticed by college coaches. While playing club lacrosse can provide excellent exposure, it is not the only way to get recruited. In this article, we will explore how to get colleges to notice you for lacrosse and how Fan Arch's recruitment websites can help with recruitment.

Start Early

The first step to getting noticed by college coaches is to start early. You should begin your recruiting process as early as your sophomore year of high school. This allows you to get a head start on the competition and gives you time to develop your skills and build a strong player profile.

Create a Player Profile

One of the most important things you can do to get noticed by college coaches is to create a player profile. A player profile is a comprehensive document that showcases your skills, academic achievements, and personal qualities. Your player profile should include your contact information, academic transcripts, athletic achievements, and personal statement.

A well-crafted player profile can make a big difference in getting noticed by college coaches. It provides them with a comprehensive overview of your skills and abilities and helps them to determine whether you are a good fit for their program.

Attend Camps and Clinics

Attending camps and clinics is another great way to get noticed by college coaches. These events provide you with the opportunity to showcase your skills and interact with college coaches in a more personal setting. Many college coaches attend camps and clinics to scout for new talent, so attending these events can significantly increase your exposure.

Contact Coaches Directly

Another way to get noticed by college coaches is to contact them directly. You can find contact information for coaches on their team websites or by reaching out to their athletic department. When contacting coaches, be sure to introduce yourself and provide them with your player profile. You can also include a brief highlight video that showcases your skills.

Showcase Personal Qualities

College coaches not only look for talented athletes but also for players who exhibit strong personal qualities. You can showcase your personal qualities by demonstrating leadership, teamwork, and a strong work ethic. Coaches want players who are coachable, hardworking, and dedicated to their sport.

Conclusion

Getting noticed by college coaches for lacrosse requires dedication, hard work, and a proactive approach. Starting early, creating a player profile, attending camps and clinics, contacting coaches directly, and showcasing personal qualities are all essential steps to take.
