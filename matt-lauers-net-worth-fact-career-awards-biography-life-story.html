Early Life of Matt Lauer

The full name of Matt Lauer is Matthew Todd Lauer. He was born on 30 December 1957. He was born to Jay Robert Lauer and Marilyn Lauer. His birthplace is New York City in the United States. He holds American nationality. He attended Scripps College of Communication, School of Media Arts and Studies. At the age of 39, he completed his undergraduate degree from Ohio University.

Personal Life of Matt Lauer

Lauer’s first wife is Nancy Alspaugh. She is a television producer and they got married in 1982. They split in 1989. Lauer then married Annette Roque, a Dutch model. They met on a blind date in 1997. They got married in October 1998 and have three children together. They are Jack, Romy, and Thijs. They separated in 2017 and their divorce is yet to be finalized.

Sexual Misconduct Allegations of Matt Lauer

NBC terminated Lauer’s contract on 29 November 2017, after he was alleged for sexual misconduct by an unidentified NBC female employee. According to her, Lauer sexually harassed her during the 2014 Winter Olympics in Sochi in Russia. She reported that Lauer continued the harassment after they returned back to New York. NBC fired Lauer without any financial settlement. Later, several other accusations went public.

Lauer publicly apologized, “Some of what is being said about me is untrue or mischaracterized, but there is enough truth in these stories to make me feel embarrassed and ashamed.”

A couple of weeks later, Today production assistant Addie Zinone made an additional accusation on Lauer. She said Lauer used his power to force her to have a sexual relationship with him, and she could not turn down Lauer because her career was in line.
