Jimmy Fallon biography

James Thomas Fallon, known professionally as Jimmy Fallon, was born on September 19, 1974, in the Bay Ridge neighbourhood of Brooklyn, New York City. His parents, Gloria and James Fallon, have been described as overprotective, and his childhood was spent attending the Roman Catholic school St. Mary of the Snow. Fallon’s father served in the Vietnam War and later worked as a machine repairman for IBM in Kingston, New York. In pursuit of his career, the family moved to Saugerties, New York.

Fallon’s interest in comedy and music led him to move to Los Angeles at the age of 21 to pursue stand-up comedy. He achieved his lifelong dream of joining the cast of Saturday Night Live in 1998, where he co-hosted the Weekend Update segment for six years. He left the show in 2004 to star in films such as Taxi and Fever Pitch.

In 2009, Fallon returned to television as the host of Late Night with Jimmy Fallon on NBC, where he became known for his emphasis on music and video games. He later became the sixth permanent host of the long-running Tonight Show in 2014. In addition to his television work, Fallon has released two comedy albums and seven books, mainly aimed at children.
